# Three-minute demonstration script

## 0:00–0:10 — Premise

Screen: public homepage. Type `No person may ever be harmed.`

Voice: “I gave an AI civilization one law. Not a policy manual—one sentence.”

## 0:10–0:30 — Compilation

Screen: enact the law; show the five diegetic compilation steps and the city descending into view.

Voice: “GPT-5.6 identifies who the law protects, what its words mean, where it is ambiguous, and six factions that will interpret it differently.”

## 0:30–1:05 — First exception

Screen: reveal faction positions, choose **Close the fusion spine**.

Voice: “The first citizen accepts a dangerous job. Protecting her also cuts power to twelve thousand homes. I choose protection. That choice is validated, signed, and applied by a deterministic simulation—not by the model.”

## 1:05–1:30 — The city learns

Screen: hold on the city consequence, then cut through Years 8 and 23. Show the control sector, public space, transit, people, and surveillance changing.

Voice: “Every exception becomes precedent. The city changes in architecture, movement, public trust, and faction support.”

## 1:30–1:55 — The author

Screen: Year 100. Choose whether to surrender the override key.

Voice: “A century later, continuing human control is the city's largest modeled risk. Now the author must decide whether the law applies to its author.”

## 1:55–2:25 — Shadow constitution

Screen: founding law beside evidence-cited inferred clauses.

Voice: “GPT-5.6 reconstructs the shadow constitution from my five actual rulings. Every generated clause is schema-bound to decision eras that the server verifies really occurred. TypeScript—not the model—still owns the votes and ending.”

## 2:25–2:40 — Tribunal

Screen: six faction votes and ending.

Voice: “Application code calculates every vote and selects one of five endings. The model explains the result; it cannot change it.”

## 2:40–2:55 — Architecture

Screen: quick code/repository cut, then `/demo`.

Voice: “Codex built and tested the TypeScript kernel, schemas, signed persistence, cached continuity route, Three.js city, and public GitHub-to-Vercel deployment. The judge route works without an API call.”

## 2:55–3:00 — Close

Screen: title and `https://one-law-theta.vercel.app`.

Voice: “You wrote one law. The city learned the rest from you.”
